Phase 01
Discovery · 发现
No Login Required
Why First

Users arrive with zero context. Requiring registration before showing any value is the #1 reason B2B platforms fail to convert. Discovery lets users immediately experience the platform's core value — global conference intelligence — with zero friction. Only after they feel the pull do we ask for commitment.

Design Logic: Value before registration. Let users feel the product before asking for anything in return. The conversion funnel starts with genuine interest, not a gate.

Benchmark: Eventbrite homepage · GDC schedule page
Includes
  • 📰
    Homepage Feed
    Curated content stream of industry news, upcoming events, and featured games — personalized over time.
  • 📅
    Conference Calendar
    Global games industry event calendar. Dual data sources: web crawler (external events) + platform-hosted events. The crawler gives immediate content depth on day one.
  • 🗺️
    Live Event Guide
    Real-time navigation during active conferences: schedules, speakers, side events, venue info. Goes live during each event window.
🪪
Phase 02
Identity · 身份
Google Sign-In Only
Why Second

After users discover value, they want to save content, follow people, and be seen. Identity unlocks the social layer. In B2B, your profile is your business card — a professional presence here carries real commercial weight at industry events.

Design Logic: Frictionless onboarding via Google Sign-In only. No email verification, no custom password. Profile doubles as a B2B business card: past events attended, talks given, availability windows for Let's Meet.

Includes
  • 🔑
    Auth (Google)
    One-click registration via Google OAuth. No password, no verification email.
  • 👤
    Profile
    Personal page with attended conferences, speaking records, and Let's Meet availability slots. Your public B2B identity.
  • 🎮
    Projects
    Game project showcase — the creative portfolio for pitching to publishers and partners.
  • 🏢
    Organizations
    Company pages — team members, projects, events organized. The institutional layer of identity.
  • ⚙️
    Settings
    Notification preferences, privacy controls, account management.
💬
Phase 03
Connection · 连接
1-to-1 First
Why Third

Identities without communication channels are isolated nodes. Once users have profiles they're proud of, they need a way to reach each other directly. The messaging system turns a directory into a network. Building this before event tools ensures the human communication layer is solid before we add event complexity.

Design Logic: Start with the simplest primitive — 1-to-1 direct messages. Rich attachments (files, business cards, meeting invites) are included from the start because B2B conversations require them. Group chat and project channels are roadmap extensions on this foundation.

Includes
  • ✉️
    Messages
    Private 1-to-1 messaging with support for: file attachments, digital business cards, and meeting invite sharing. Foundation for future group channels and project rooms.
Roadmap Extensions:
Group messaging · Project channels · Read receipts · Message reactions · Bot integrations
🤝
Phase 04
Meetup · 小型活动
MVP for Phase 5
Why Fourth

Users are no longer purely passive attendees — they want to convene their own gatherings. Meetup is the lightweight, user-generated event layer. It also serves as a critical product validation step: before we build the full enterprise Events engine (Phase 5), we prove the event publishing flow with the simplest possible version.

Design Logic: Inspired by Meetup.com — minimal barrier to publishing. Events under 50 attendees require no Organizer account. This keeps the product accessible to indie developers and small studios, not just conference corporations.

Includes
  • ➕
    MeetupPublish
    Any user can publish a small gathering — drinks after GDC, a developer meetup, a demo showcase. Under 50 attendees: no Organizer account required.
  • 📋
    EventPublicPage
    Public-facing event detail page — shareable link, RSVP, attendee list. Reused in Phase 5 as the foundation for large-event public pages.
🎪
Phase 05
Events · 展会全功能
12 Organizer Modules
Why Fifth

Phase 4 proved the event publishing flow at small scale. Now we expand to professional large-scale conferences — GDC-tier events with thousands of attendees, multiple tracks, exhibitor halls, and awards ceremonies. The key architectural decision: every B2B Organizer feature has a strict 1-to-1 B2C attendee counterpart.

Core Design Logic

B2B generates, B2C consumes. Organizers configure every feature; attendees experience the output. This strict pairing prevents orphaned features — if there's no attendee-facing interface, the Organizer module doesn't ship. Features unlock progressively based on event scale (tiered access model).

The tiered unlock model: small events (<500 attendees) get modules 1–5. Mid-scale (500–5,000) unlock 6–9. Enterprise-tier events (>5,000) unlock all 12. This prevents Organizer overwhelm and lets the platform grow with the event.

Phase 06
Sponsorship · 赞助商
Revenue Accelerator
Why Sixth

Once events reach meaningful scale, sponsorship becomes the platform's most significant revenue lever — both for the platform and for Organizers. Sponsors are a unique user type: they're simultaneously buyers (purchasing visibility) and exhibitors (presenting their brand). This dual role requires a dedicated module.

Design Logic: Flexible package tiers (Title / Gold / Silver / Bronze) with configurable benefits per tier. Sponsors can customize their rights. Early introduction of the sponsorship module is a deliberate monetization strategy — revenue doesn't wait until Phase 7.

B2B ↔ B2C Split
  • ⚙️
    6.1 · SponsorshipManagement (B2B)
    Organizer configures sponsorship packages: tier names, pricing, benefit lists (logo placement, booth priority, speaking slots, networking access), availability limits.
  • 🛍️
    6.2 · SponsorExplore (B2C)
    Sponsors browse available packages across events, compare tier benefits, and purchase directly. Self-serve reduces Organizer sales overhead significantly.

💼
Phase 07
Commerce · 商务
Deal Closing Layer
Why Seventh

The platform's ultimate value proposition is not just connecting people — it's closing deals. The first six phases build the trust, identity, and network required for high-stakes publishing negotiations. Phase 7 converts those relationships into signed contracts.

Design Logic: LinkedIn built the world's largest professional network but deliberately keeps commerce off the platform. We do the opposite — build the network (Phases 1–6) then open a full deal pipeline (Phase 7). The complete arc: discover → connect → negotiate → sign → publish.

Analogy: LinkedIn for social graph · AngelList for deal flow · DocuSign for execution — unified in one platform
Includes
  • 📊
    ProjectBiz
    Business-layer extension of a game project: financial projections, revenue expectations, publishing territory requirements, deal terms overview. Shown only to invited parties under NDA.
  • 🔄
    DealNegotiation
    Structured deal pipeline tracker: NDA → Term Sheet → Contract. Timestamped milestones, comment threads, version history per deal.
  • ✍️
    ContractSign
    In-platform e-signature for finalized contracts. No need to export to DocuSign — the deal closes where it was negotiated.
  • 🏛️
    DealRoom
    Unified private workspace per deal: all documents, messages, milestones, and signatories in one secure room. The command center for a publishing partnership.
Design Philosophy

From Discovery to Deal — A Trust-First Funnel

The 7-phase sequence is not arbitrary. It mirrors how trust is actually built in professional relationships: you discover, you identify yourself, you communicate, you gather, you engage at scale, you attract investment, and finally — when trust is deep enough — you transact.

Each phase creates the precondition for the next. You can't have meaningful Commerce (Phase 7) without the trust network built in Phases 1–6. You can't have Events (Phase 5) without the identity infrastructure of Phase 2 or the messaging of Phase 3. The sequence is load-bearing.
